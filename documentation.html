Project Documentation

Design Process

I began by exploring a dataset of movies and thinking about what it means for a movie to be considered "top." At first, I considered creating a simple bar chart showing the highest rated movies. However, I realized that different metrics like popularity and vote count could produce very different results.

I experimented with different ideas, including separate graphs and combined views, but ultimately chose an interactive design where users can switch between metrics using buttons.

Rationale of Design Choices

I chose a bar chart because it allows for easy comparison between movies. The interactive buttons allow users to redefine what "top" means, making the visualization more engaging.

I kept the color consistent to avoid confusion and added labels and axes so the data is easy to read. The layout was kept simple to keep the focus on the data.

Findings

The visualization shows that the definition of a "top" movie changes depending on the metric used. Highly rated movies tend to be critically acclaimed classics, while the most popular movies are often newer or trending. Movies with the highest vote counts are typically widely viewed mainstream films.

This demonstrates that popularity and quality are not always the same and that different metrics highlight different types of success.
